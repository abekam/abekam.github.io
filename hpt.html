Momentum Boost

Performance Intervention for Newly Hired Sales Agents

This project describes efforts to improve the performance of newly hired sales agents. The project identifies performance gaps and suggests eight interventions to attain organizational goals for customer experience and sales
Background

The director of GLM noticed a drop in the performance of newly hired sales agents. The Average Order Value (AOV) and call quality have decreased significantly from last year. New sales agents often miss some steps of the process and are slower in answering customer requests. The direcrtor indicated a desire to assess the current situation and suggest improvements.

The main performers are the newly hired call center sales agents, however, direct supervisors act as secondary perfomers to assist in the implementation of the suggested interventions.
The main business need of this project is to generate revenue by optimizing the performance of the new sales agents in the first three months of employment to reach acceptable sales and quality levels.
